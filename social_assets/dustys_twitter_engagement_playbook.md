# Dusty's Motorcycle Service & Repair — X/Twitter Engagement Playbook

**Purpose:** Build a real-time conversation presence for Dusty's on X/Twitter without sounding spammy, corporate, or like a dealership.

**Core positioning:** Orange County's no-BS motorcycle mechanic. Text a photo, get a straight answer.

---

## X/Twitter Role

X should be used for:

- fast rider education,
- local Orange County conversation,
- motorcycle safety reminders,
- used-bike buyer advice,
- real-time replies to rider questions,
- connecting with local shops, clubs, veterans, and riding communities.

Do **not** use X as a broadcast-only channel.

---

## Voice Rules

Sound like:

- a helpful mechanic,
- direct but not rude,
- local and practical,
- safety-conscious,
- human.

Avoid:

- corporate slogans,
- generic motivational content,
- dunking on competitors,
- diagnosing safety-critical issues with certainty from one photo,
- thread spam.

## Reply Templates

### Weird noise

```text
Hard to price from “weird noise” alone.

Better first text to any mechanic:
- year/make/model
- when it happens
- what changed recently
- short video with sound
- whether it still rides

If it’s brakes/tires/steering/leak related, don’t ride it until it’s checked.
```

### Used bike buying

```text
Used-bike rule: see it cold.

A warm engine can hide hard starts, smoke, idle issues, weak battery, or noises.

Before cash changes hands: VIN/title, tires, fork seals, chain/sprockets, brakes, leaks, crash signs.
```

### Price question

```text
A real quote needs scope.

Known service? Bike + parts usually gets you close.
Symptom? Diagnose first or you’re guessing.

That’s how riders get sold the wrong repair.
```

### Local recommendation

```text
If you’re near Orange, ask any shop:
1. Do you work on my exact bike?
2. Do you quote before starting?
3. Can I text photos/video first?

Disclosure: I’m affiliated with Dusty’s in Orange. Even if you don’t use us, those questions matter.
```

## Crisis / Negative Comment Protocol

### If someone complains publicly

1. Acknowledge without arguing.
2. Move details to DM/text/phone.
3. Do not disclose customer info.
4. Do not blame the customer publicly.
5. Follow up once resolved.

Template:

```text
Sorry to hear this. I don’t want to discuss repair details publicly, but I do want to understand what happened. Please text/call the shop with your name and bike so we can look into it directly.
```
