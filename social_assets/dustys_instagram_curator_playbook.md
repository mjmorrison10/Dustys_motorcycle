# Dusty's Motorcycle Service & Repair — Instagram Curator Playbook

**Goal:** Turn Dusty's Instagram into a visual trust engine for Orange County riders: real shop work, no-BS diagnosis, ride-ready education, and text-first leads.

**Campaign anchor:** Text a Photo. Get a Straight Answer.

---

## Instagram Aesthetic System

### Visual identity

- **Core look:** real garage documentary, black/orange/steel, warm shop light, close-up mechanical texture.
- **Do not chase:** glossy dealership ads, fake influencer energy, generic AI motorcycle glamour.
- **Use often:** hands, tools, chains, brake pads, tires, bike lifts, checkered floor, shop doorway light, real bikes.
- **Color anchors:** black, white, steel gray, Dusty's orange.
- **Grid feel:** alternating close-up repair detail, bike/shop proof, text education card.

### Feed pattern

Use a repeating 3-post rhythm:

1. **Proof post** — real bike / real repair.
2. **Education post** — carousel, checklist, or repair lesson.
3. **Trust post** — review, no-upsell promise, veteran-owned/local proof.

This creates a grid that balances capability, value, and trust.

## Format Mix

### Reels — 40%

Best for reach and education.

Formats:

- “Would you ride this?”
- “What this noise might mean”
- “60-second pre-ride check”
- “Used-bike red flag”
- “Before/after repair detail”

### Carousels — 30%

Best for saves and education.

Formats:

- checklist posts,
- buyer red flags,
- step-by-step “what to text,”
- “what can wait vs. what needs repair now.”

### Single image posts — 20%

Best for proof and trust.

Formats:

- bike on lift,
- part close-up,
- shop shot,
- quote/review card.

### Stories — 10%+

Best for daily presence and DMs.

Formats:

- polls,
- Q&A sticker,
- shop moment,
- “text me a photo” reminder,
- repost customer tags.

## Community Management Rules

- Reply to comments within 24 hours when possible.
- Like/reply to local rider comments first.
- Move diagnosis into DMs/text: “Text the year/make/model and a photo.”
- Ask permission before posting identifiable customer bikes, plates, or faces.
- Never diagnose safety-critical issues definitively from a comment.
- Pin comments that reinforce trust: “quote before we wrench,” “text a photo,” “Orange, CA.”
