# Dusty's X/Twitter Thread Bank

## Thread 1 — What to Text a Mechanic

**Hook:**

```text
If your bike starts making a weird noise, don’t text a mechanic “how much?” yet.
```

**Thread:**

```text
If your bike starts making a weird noise, don’t text a mechanic “how much?” yet.

Text this instead:

1/ Year / make / model.

“2018 Honda CBR500R” is 10x more useful than “my sportbike.”

2/ What changed?

Noise, leak, won’t start, brake feel, chain slap, wobble, smoke, smell.

Be specific, even if you don’t know the mechanical term.

3/ When does it happen?

Idle?
Acceleration?
Braking?
Turning?
Over bumps?
Cold start?
After warming up?

This tells the mechanic where to look first.

4/ Send a photo or short video.

A 10-second video with sound can save 10 back-and-forth texts.

5/ Say whether it still rides.

And if it involves brakes, tires, steering, throttle, fuel, or oil leaks — don’t ride it until it’s checked.

Useful context beats perfect mechanic language.

Text a photo. Get a straight answer.
```

## Thread 2 — Used Bike Red Flags

**Hook:**

```text
Buying a used motorcycle? Show up before the bike is warm.
```

**Thread:**

```text
Buying a used motorcycle? Show up before the bike is warm.

A cold start tells you things a warm engine can hide.

1/ Watch the start.

Hard cranking, smoke, weak battery, rough idle, or weird engine noise all matter.

2/ Check VIN and title.

If the seller can’t make the paperwork make sense, don’t let excitement override that.

3/ Look at tire date codes.

Good tread doesn’t matter much if the tire is old, cracked, or flat-spotted.

4/ Fork seals.

Oil on the fork tubes can turn into a real repair cost fast.

5/ Chain and sprockets.

Hooked teeth, tight spots, rust, or kinked links change the real price of the bike.

6/ Brakes.

Pads, rotors, lever feel, fluid condition.

Brakes are not a “fix later maybe” system.

7/ Crash signs.

Bars, levers, pegs, engine cases, plastics, subframe, mismatched parts.

If the seller won’t allow a mechanic inspection, price it like there’s a hidden problem — or walk.
```

## Thread 3 — Before Santiago Canyon

**Hook:**

```text
Before you ride Santiago Canyon, give the bike 60 seconds.
```

**Thread:**

```text
Before you ride Santiago Canyon, give the bike 60 seconds.

1/ Tires.
Pressure, tread, cracking, nails, sidewall issues.

2/ Brakes.
Firm lever, no grinding, no fluid leak, no weird pulsing.

3/ Chain.
Slack, lube, tight spots, sprocket teeth.

4/ Fluids.
Oil, coolant if applicable, brake fluid, fuel smell.

5/ Controls.
Throttle snaps back. Clutch feels normal. Bars turn without cables binding.

6/ Lights.
Headlight, brake light, turn signals, horn.

If something feels off, don’t “see if it clears up” on the ride.

Text a photo or video first.
```

## Thread 4 — Quote Before We Wrench

**Hook:**

```text
A good mechanic should be able to tell you what’s required now and what can wait.
```

**Thread:**

```text
A good mechanic should be able to tell you what’s required now and what can wait.

That’s the difference between service and upsell.

Required now:
Safety issues.
Failed parts.
Problems that can cause more damage.

Can wait:
Wear items with life left.
Preferences.
Nice-to-haves.
Non-urgent maintenance.

If a shop can’t explain which is which, pause.

Ask:
“What did you find?”
“How did you confirm it?”
“What happens if I wait?”
“What are parts vs labor?”
“Will you call before doing anything extra?”

Quote before wrenching is not a slogan.

It’s how trust survives the repair bill.
```
