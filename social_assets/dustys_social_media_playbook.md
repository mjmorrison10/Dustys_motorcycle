# Dusty's Motorcycle Service & Repair — Social Media Playbook

**Purpose:** Give Dusty a practical, no-BS social strategy that turns real shop work into trust, local visibility, and text-first leads.

**Primary platforms:** Google Business Profile, Instagram, Facebook.  
**Secondary / later:** YouTube Shorts, TikTok, Nextdoor, local riding groups.

---

## Core Social Positioning

> Veteran-owned, no-BS motorcycle repair in Orange, CA. Text a photo, get a straight answer. No phantom repairs. No upsells.

Dusty should not try to look like a dealership or influencer shop. The social advantage is real work, real bikes, real local riders, and straight explanations.

## Content Pillars

### 1. Real Repairs

**Goal:** Prove capability and authenticity.

Examples:
- bike on lift,
- chain/sprocket replacement,
- brake pads,
- tire mount/balance,
- carb work,
- fork seals,
- dirt bike / ATV / vintage bike detail.

CTA:

```text
Something look familiar? Text Dusty a photo.
```

---

### 2. No-BS Diagnosis

**Goal:** Reinforce trust and anti-upsell positioning.

Examples:
- “This did not need a full rebuild.”
- “Here’s what can wait.”
- “Here’s the part that actually failed.”
- “Quote before we wrench.”

CTA:

```text
Not sure what’s wrong? Text the year/make/model and a photo.
```

---

### 3. Ride-Ready Safety

**Goal:** Own Santiago Canyon / weekend ride prep.

Examples:
- tire pressure check,
- chain slack,
- brake feel,
- oil/coolant check,
- throttle snap-back,
- lights/horn.

CTA:

```text
Heading toward Santiago Canyon? Check this before you ride.
```

---

### 4. Used-Bike Buyer Protection

**Goal:** Drive pre-purchase inspection leads.

Examples:
- title/VIN check,
- tire date code,
- fork seal leak,
- cold start warning signs,
- seller red flags.

CTA:

```text
Buying used? Send Dusty the listing before you hand over cash.
```

---

### 5. Local Shop / Veteran-Owned

**Goal:** Build community and local identity.

Examples:
- Orange Traffic Circle location references,
- behind-the-scenes shop photos,
- veteran-owned trust signal,
- customer bikes without exposing private info,
- local ride prep.

CTA:

```text
Orange riders — save Dusty’s number before you need it.
```

## Posting Cadence

### Minimum viable cadence

| Platform | Frequency | Content |
|---|---:|---|
| Google Business Profile | 2x/week | photos, offers, ride-prep posts |
| Instagram feed/reels | 3x/week | repair clips, before/after, tips |
| Facebook | 2x/week | repost IG + local/community notes |
| Stories | 3–5x/week | quick shop moments, polls, reminders |

### 15-minute workflow

1. Take one shop photo or 10-second video.
2. Add a simple caption from this playbook.
3. Post to Instagram.
4. Cross-post to Facebook.
5. Once or twice per week, post the same theme to Google Business Profile.

## 30-Day Campaign Theme

**Campaign:** “Text a Photo. Get a Straight Answer.”

**Goal:** Train local riders that texting Dusty is the first move when they notice a problem.

**Primary CTA:**

```text
Text Dusty: 714-787-9453
```

**Success metrics:**

- 12+ posts published,
- 8+ Google Business Profile photos/posts,
- 10+ repair/story clips captured,
- 5+ inbound leads mention “saw it on Instagram/Facebook/Google,”
- increased SMS lead quality: more messages include year/make/model + issue/photo.

---

## Engagement Rules

- Reply to comments within 24 hours when possible.
- Never argue publicly with bad-faith commenters.
- Move diagnosis questions to text: “Text me the year/make/model and a photo.”
- Ask permission before posting identifiable customer bikes, plates, or faces.
- Blur license plates when needed.
- Avoid promising safety clearance from a photo alone.
