# Dusty's Motorcycle Service & Repair — Sales Coaching Playbook

**Purpose:** Coach Dusty or any helper/apprentice to turn inbound calls/texts into clear next steps, better-qualified jobs, and fewer missed opportunities.

**Core idea:** In a small repair shop, sales coaching is not about “closing harder.” It is about responding clearly, asking better diagnostic questions, setting expectations, and following up.

---

## Coaching Philosophy

Dusty's lead process should be coached like a craft skill:

1. Observe the interaction.
2. Identify one behavior to improve.
3. Practice the better version.
4. Check the next real interaction.

Do not try to fix everything at once.

---

## The Five Behaviors to Coach

### 1. Fast acknowledgement

**Target behavior:** every inbound text/form lead gets a response or acknowledgement as quickly as possible during shop hours.

Good first reply:

```text
Got it. Send year/make/model, what changed, and one clear photo/video if you can. I’ll tell you if it sounds like quick service, diagnosis, or something I need to see in person.
```

Coach against:

- waiting until the end of day,
- asking only “what bike?” without next step,
- giving a vague “bring it by” when more triage is needed.

---

### 2. Complete triage

**Target behavior:** collect enough information to classify the lead.

Minimum fields:

- year / make / model,
- issue or service needed,
- rideability/safety concern,
- photo/video when useful,
- timeline,
- source.

Coach against:

- quoting with incomplete info,
- failing to ask if the bike is rideable,
- missing safety red flags.

---

### 3. Safety boundary

**Target behavior:** never clear a bike as safe from vague text/photo info.

Good language:

```text
If it involves brakes, tires, steering, throttle, fuel, or an oil leak, I wouldn’t ride it until it’s checked. Send a photo/video and I’ll tell you the safest next step.
```

Coach against:

- “yeah you’re probably fine,”
- diagnosing safety-critical issues with certainty from one photo,
- telling riders to test ride a worsening problem.

---

### 4. Quote-before-wrenching

**Target behavior:** every customer understands no work starts without approval.

Good language:

```text
I’ll look at it first and give you the plan before work starts. If I find something else, I’ll call/text before touching it.
```

Coach against:

- unclear approval language,
- scope creep without communication,
- “we’ll figure it out” when the customer is worried about cost.

---

### 5. Follow-up discipline

**Target behavior:** no qualified lead goes cold without at least one follow-up.

Follow-up template:

```text
Hey, just checking whether you still need help with the [bike/issue]. If you want, send a photo/video and I’ll tell you the best next step.
```

Coach against:

- assuming silence means no interest,
- failing to follow up on inspection/checklist leads,
- not asking for review/referral after completed work.

---

## Weekly Coaching Cadence

### 15-minute weekly lead review

Review:

- number of inbound leads,
- missed calls/texts,
- response time,
- leads with complete bike info,
- leads with photo/video,
- quote approvals,
- no-shows,
- completed jobs,
- reviews requested.

Ask:

1. Which lead was easiest to convert and why?
2. Which lead went cold and why?
3. Where did we guess instead of asking one more question?
4. Which customer needed clearer expectations?
5. What one phrase should we practice this week?

## Text/Call Coaching Scorecard

Score each interaction 1–5.

| Competency | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acknowledgement speed | late/no reply | same day | fast and clear |
| Triage quality | vague | some details | year/make/model + issue + timeline + photo/video |
| Safety handling | risky | cautious but vague | clear safety boundary |
| Next step | unclear | general | specific next step with owner/time |
| Trust language | transactional | polite | quote-before-wrenching/no-upsell reinforced |
| Follow-up | none | ad hoc | documented and timely |

---

## Coaching Questions

Use these instead of lectures:

- What did we know before quoting?
- What did we still not know?
- What was the safest next step for the rider?
- Did the customer understand what happens before work starts?
- Did we ask for the photo/video at the right time?
- What would have made this lead easier to handle?
- What phrase should we use next time?

## Role Plays

### Role Play 1 — Vague noise

Customer:

```text
My bike is making a weird noise. How much to fix it?
```

Goal:

- do not quote blindly,
- ask for year/make/model,
- ask when noise happens,
- ask for video,
- set next step.

---

### Role Play 2 — Safety concern

Customer:

```text
My brake lever feels soft but I need to ride tomorrow. Is it okay?
```

Goal:

- avoid clearing it remotely,
- set safety boundary,
- ask for photo/video,
- recommend inspection before riding.

---

### Role Play 3 — Price shopper

Customer:

```text
Another shop said they can do it cheaper.
```

Goal:

- acknowledge comparison,
- ask whether scope/parts/labor match,
- reinforce quote-before-wrenching,
- avoid trashing competitor.

---

### Role Play 4 — Used bike buyer

Customer:

```text
I’m looking at a used bike on Marketplace. Seller says it runs great. Should I buy it?
```

Goal:

- ask for listing/year/mileage/title,
- recommend cold start and inspection,
- offer pre-purchase inspection.

## Pipeline Review for a Small Shop

### Lead stages

1. New inquiry
2. Info requested
3. Quote/diagnosis recommended
4. Appointment/drop-off planned
5. Active job
6. Completed
7. Review requested
8. Referral candidate
9. Lost / no response

### Review question per stage

| Stage | Coaching Question |
|---|---|
| New inquiry | Did we acknowledge it? |
| Info requested | What info is still missing? |
| Quote/diagnosis recommended | Did they understand why diagnosis is needed? |
| Appointment/drop-off planned | Is there a date/time and expectation? |
| Active job | Has scope changed? Did customer approve? |
| Completed | Did we ask how the bike feels? |
| Review requested | Did we send the review link? |
| Referral candidate | Did they have a good enough experience to ask? |
| Lost/no response | Did we follow up once? |

## Coaching Metrics

Track weekly:

- inbound leads,
- response time,
- complete triage rate,
- photo/video capture rate,
- quote approval rate,
- diagnostic approval rate,
- no-show rate,
- job completion count,
- review request rate,
- review acquisition rate,
- referral asks.

Targets after process is working:

| Metric | Target |
|---|---:|
| Same-day response during shop hours | 90%+ |
| Complete triage rate | 75%+ |
| Photo/video included on text leads | 50%+ |
| Qualified lead follow-up | 90%+ |
| Review request after completed positive job | 80%+ |
