# Dusty's Motorcycle Service & Repair — Objection & Competitive Battlecards

## Battlecard 1 — “The dealership can do it.”

**Acknowledge:**

```text
Dealerships can be the right call for warranty or brand-specific factory work.
```

**Reframe:**

```text
If you want a local independent shop that explains the issue, quotes before wrenching, and avoids surprise add-ons, that’s where Dusty’s fits.
```

**Question:**

```text
Is this warranty work, or are you paying out of pocket?
```

---

## Battlecard 2 — “Another shop quoted less.”

**Acknowledge:**

```text
Totally fair to compare quotes.
```

**Reframe:**

```text
Make sure both quotes include the same parts, labor, diagnosis, and scope. A cheaper quote that leaves out the real issue usually costs more later.
```

**Question:**

```text
Did they separate what’s required now from what can wait?
```

---

## Battlecard 3 — “Can you tell me the price by text?”

**Acknowledge:**

```text
I get why you want a number first.
```

**Reframe:**

```text
For known services, we can usually get close once we know the bike and parts. For symptoms, guessing is how riders get sold the wrong repair.
```

**Question:**

```text
Can you send year/make/model, the issue, and a photo or short video?
```

---

## Battlecard 4 — “I can do it myself.”

**Acknowledge:**

```text
Some maintenance is absolutely DIY-friendly if you have the tools and confidence.
```

**Reframe:**

```text
Brakes, tires, chains, fuel, electrical, and steering issues are where mistakes can get expensive or unsafe.
```

**Question:**

```text
What part are you planning to do, and what tools/specs do you have?
```

---

## Battlecard 5 — “Is it safe to ride?”

**Acknowledge:**

```text
That’s the right question to ask.
```

**Reframe:**

```text
If it involves brakes, tires, steering, throttle, fuel, or oil leaks, I can’t clear it safely from text alone. Send a photo/video and I’ll tell you the safest next step.
```

**Question:**

```text
What exactly changed, and when does it happen?
```
