# Dusty's Motorcycle Service & Repair — Support Responder Playbook

**Purpose:** Provide repeatable, channel-specific support responses for texts, calls, forms, DMs, public comments, and follow-ups.

**Support promise:** respond fast, ask for the right context, avoid unsafe guesses, and make the next step obvious.

---

## Support Priorities

1. Safety-critical issues first.
2. Post-service concerns second.
3. Active job questions third.
4. New repair/inspection inquiries fourth.
5. General questions and content requests fifth.

---

## Response SLA Targets

| Channel | Target First Response | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| SMS/text | same shop day; faster when possible | primary lead channel |
| Phone voicemail | same shop day | call back or text if appropriate |
| Website form | same shop day | blocked until endpoint live |
| Instagram/Facebook DM | within 24 hours | move repair diagnosis to text |
| Public comments | within 24 hours | do not diagnose publicly |
| Safety concern | ASAP when seen | do not clear remotely |
| Complaint/post-service concern | ASAP / owner escalation | no review ask until resolved |

## Universal Intake Macro

```text
Thanks for reaching out. Send year/make/model, what changed, and one clear photo or short video if you can. We’ll tell you if it sounds like quick service, diagnosis, or something Dusty needs to see in person.
```

---

## Channel-Specific Macros

### SMS — new repair inquiry

```text
Got it. What year/make/model is the bike, and when does the issue happen — idle, acceleration, braking, turning, bumps, hot/cold? A short video with sound helps a lot.
```

### SMS — safety concern

```text
I don’t want to clear that as safe by text. If it involves brakes, tires, steering, throttle, fuel, or oil leak, don’t ride it until it’s checked. Send a photo/video and Dusty will tell you the safest next step.
```

### SMS — price question

```text
For known services we can usually get close once we know the bike and parts. For symptoms, Dusty needs to diagnose before giving a real price. Send year/make/model, issue, and a photo/video so we can point you the right way.
```

### Website form acknowledgement

```text
Thanks — we received your message. If you can, text 714-787-9453 with your bike year/make/model and a photo/video of the issue. That usually helps Dusty reply faster than email alone.
```

### Instagram/Facebook DM

```text
Thanks for the message. For repair questions, text Dusty directly at 714-787-9453 with year/make/model, what changed, and a photo/video. That keeps the details in one place and makes it easier to reply.
```

### Public comment

```text
Hard to diagnose safely from a comment. Text Dusty the year/make/model and a photo or short video: 714-787-9453. If it involves brakes, tires, steering, throttle, fuel, or oil leak, don’t ride it until it’s checked.
```

## First-Contact Resolution Checklist

A support reply is complete when it includes:

- [ ] acknowledgement,
- [ ] bike context requested or confirmed,
- [ ] issue/timing requested or confirmed,
- [ ] safety boundary if relevant,
- [ ] next step,
- [ ] expectation about quote/diagnosis,
- [ ] owner/follow-up if escalation is needed.

---

## Follow-Up Rules

### New qualified lead

If no reply after 24–48 hours:

```text
Hey, just checking whether you still need help with the bike. If yes, send the year/make/model and a photo/video of the issue and we’ll point you to the best next step.
```

### Post-service concern

Follow up until resolved or clearly handed to Dusty.

### Review request

Only after positive check-in.

---

## Knowledge Base Improvement Loop

If the same question appears 3+ times in a month, create or update a FAQ/guide/social post.

Common topics to watch:

- how much will it cost,
- is it safe to ride,
- what to text,
- do you work on my bike,
- used-bike inspection,
- tire/brake/chain warning signs,
- hours/location.
