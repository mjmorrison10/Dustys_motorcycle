# Dusty's Motorcycle Service & Repair — Customer Service Playbook

**Purpose:** Give anyone answering Dusty’s phone/text/form/DM a consistent, friendly, no-BS way to help riders.

**Core support promise:** Every rider should feel heard, safe, and clear on the next step.

---

## Service Voice

Sound like:

- human,
- direct,
- calm,
- helpful,
- mechanically practical,
- never corporate.

Avoid:

- “Dear valued customer,”
- “policy says,”
- blaming the rider,
- guessing final prices,
- clearing safety issues remotely,
- arguing by text.

---

## Standard Greeting

### Phone

```text
Dusty’s Motorcycle Service & Repair, this is [Name]. How can I help you with the bike?
```

### Text

```text
Thanks for reaching out to Dusty’s. Send the year/make/model, what happened, and one clear photo or short video if you can. We’ll tell you the best next step.
```

### Form reply

```text
Thanks — we got your message. To help Dusty respond faster, please send the year/make/model, what changed, and a photo or short video if you have one.
```

---

## Inquiry Categories

| Category | What It Sounds Like | First Response |
|---|---|---|
| Quick service | oil, tires, brakes, chain, maintenance | collect bike info + parts/scope |
| Diagnostic | weird noise, leak, no-start, electrical | ask for symptoms + photo/video |
| Safety concern | brakes, tires, steering, throttle, fuel/oil leak | do not clear to ride; recommend inspection |
| Pre-purchase | “Should I buy this bike?” | ask for listing/photos + title/cold start info |
| Pricing | “How much?” | explain pricing depends on scope; ask for details |
| Complaint | “Something is wrong after service” | acknowledge, gather details, escalate to Dusty |
| Review/referral | happy customer | thank them + send review/referral link |

---

## Safety Boundary Script

Use for brakes, tires, steering, throttle, fuel leaks, oil leaks, wobble, or worsening noise.

```text
I don’t want to clear that as safe from a text. If it involves brakes, tires, steering, throttle, fuel, or an oil leak, don’t ride it until it’s checked. Send a photo/video and we’ll tell you the safest next step.
```

---

## Pricing Question Script

```text
I get wanting a number first. For known services, we can usually get closer once we know the bike and parts. For symptoms, Dusty needs to diagnose before giving a real price — guessing is how riders get sold the wrong repair.

Send year/make/model, what happened, and a photo/video if you can.
```

---

## Complaint Handling

### Step 1 — Acknowledge

```text
I’m sorry you’re dealing with that. I don’t want you riding around worried, and I want Dusty to understand exactly what’s happening.
```

### Step 2 — Clarify

Ask:

- What bike?
- What service was done?
- What are you noticing now?
- When did it start?
- Can you send a photo/video?
- Is the bike currently safe to move, or should it stay parked?

### Step 3 — Escalate

```text
I’m going to get this in front of Dusty directly. Please send the photo/video and the best number to reach you. We’ll follow up with the next step.
```

### Never

- argue,
- blame,
- diagnose defensively,
- ask for a review,
- promise free work before Dusty reviews it,
- discuss sensitive details publicly.

---

## Public Social Complaint Reply

```text
Sorry to hear this. I don’t want to discuss repair details publicly, but I do want Dusty to look into it. Please text/call the shop with your name and bike so we can understand what happened and follow up directly.
```

---

## Review Ask Script

Use only after the customer confirms things feel good.

```text
Glad to hear the bike feels right. If Dusty treated you fairly, would you mind leaving a quick Google review? It helps local riders find an honest shop.

[Google Review Link]
```

---

## Referral Script

```text
Appreciate you. If you know another rider who needs a straight-answer mechanic in Orange, feel free to send them Dusty’s number: 714-787-9453.

Best first text is year/make/model + what happened + a photo or short video.
```

---

## Escalation Rules

Escalate immediately to Dusty if:

- safety concern,
- unresolved post-service issue,
- angry customer,
- legal threat,
- public complaint,
- pricing dispute,
- possible warranty/rework issue,
- bike cannot be safely ridden/moved,
- customer says they are going to post a negative review.

---

## Closing Every Interaction

End with:

```text
You’re good — we’ll watch for your photo/video and get you the next step.
```

or:

```text
Thanks for the details. Dusty will look at this and we’ll follow up with the next step.
```

or:

```text
Appreciate you reaching out. Don’t ride it if it feels unsafe — send the photo/video first.
```
