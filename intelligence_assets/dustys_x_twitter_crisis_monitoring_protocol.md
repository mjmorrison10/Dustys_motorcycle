# Dusty's X/Twitter Crisis Monitoring Protocol

**Purpose:** Define what to do if X/Twitter produces a reputation risk for Dusty's.

---

## Severity Levels

### Level 1 — Noise

One low-engagement complaint or vague negative mention.

Action:

- log URL,
- monitor,
- do not overreact.

### Level 2 — Support Issue

Identifiable customer concern or unresolved service complaint.

Action:

- acknowledge calmly,
- move details to private channel,
- notify Dusty,
- document resolution.

Response template:

```text
Sorry to hear this. I don’t want to discuss repair details publicly, but I do want to understand what happened. Please text/call the shop with your name and bike so we can look into it directly.
```

### Level 3 — Reputation Risk

Negative post gains meaningful replies/reposts or contains specific allegation.

Action:

- screenshot / save URL,
- collect timeline,
- pause promotional posting if needed,
- owner reviews facts,
- respond once with calm factual language,
- avoid argument loops.

### Level 4 — Misinformation / Safety Risk

False claim about address, hours, pricing, safety, warranty, or misconduct spreads.

Action:

- escalate immediately,
- compile evidence,
- correct factual info once,
- direct to official site/contact,
- consult legal/compliance if serious.

---

## Response Principles

- Be calm.
- Do not blame the customer publicly.
- Do not reveal private repair details.
- Do not argue thread-by-thread.
- Correct facts, then move to private resolution.
- After resolution, document what changed operationally.

---

## Evidence Pack

For any Level 2+ issue, save:

- original URL,
- screenshots if allowed,
- timestamp UTC,
- author handle,
- exact claim,
- known customer/job context,
- internal response owner,
- public response copy,
- resolution outcome.
