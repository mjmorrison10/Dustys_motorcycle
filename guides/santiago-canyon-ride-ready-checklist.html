Santiago Canyon Ride-Ready Checklist

Before you head toward Santiago Canyon, check the basics that keep a fun ride from turning into a tow: tires, brakes, chain, fluids, controls, lights, and the warning signs that mean “don’t ride it yet.”

Dusty's rule: If brakes, tires, steering, or throttle feel wrong, do not “see if it clears up.” Text a photo/video or bring it in before the ride.

60-second driveway check

  • Tires are visibly inflated, not cracked, and have enough tread.
  • No fresh oil, coolant, fuel, fork oil, or brake fluid under the bike.
  • Brake lever and pedal feel firm, not spongy.
  • Throttle snaps closed when released.
  • Clutch engages normally and cable/hydraulic feel is consistent.
  • Headlight, brake light, turn signals, and horn work.

Tires & wheels

Check before every canyon ride

  • Cold tire pressure matches your bike/tire recommendation.
  • No nails, cords, sidewall bubbles, or flat spots.
  • Valve stems are not cracked or leaking.
  • Wheels spin without grinding or wobble.

Do not ride if...

  • Tire is losing air.
  • You see cords or sidewall cracking.
  • Front end shakes or bars wobble.
  • A wheel bearing growls, clicks, or feels loose.

Brakes, chain & controls

  • Brake pads have material left and rotors are not deeply grooved.
  • Brake lever does not pull back to the grip.
  • Chain slack is in spec and chain is lubricated.
  • Sprocket teeth are not hooked or sharp.
  • Throttle, clutch, and brake cables/hoses move freely with bars turned both ways.
  • Mirrors are tight and adjusted before you leave.

Fluids & heat

  • Oil level is correct with bike checked according to your owner's manual.
  • Coolant level is in range on liquid-cooled bikes.
  • Brake fluid is above minimum and not dark/dirty.
  • No fuel smell or drips after the bike sits.
  • Fan cycles normally in traffic, if applicable.

Pack before you ride

  • License, registration, insurance, and roadside assistance info.
  • Phone mount or secure pocket, fully charged.
  • Water, clear visor/glasses, and weather-appropriate layers.
  • Basic tire gauge and small tool kit if you carry one.
  • Tell someone your route if you are riding alone.

Text Dusty if you notice...

Noise / vibration

  • Grinding when braking.
  • Chain slap, clunking, or tight spots.
  • New front-end shake or rear wobble.
  • Metallic ticking, knocking, or scraping.

Leaks / smell / heat

  • Fuel smell.
  • Oil on fork tubes or under engine.
  • Coolant smell or temp climbing.
  • Smoke that does not clear after startup.

Not sure it’s safe?

Text Dusty the year/make/model, what changed, and one photo or short video. A quick check before the canyon is cheaper than a tow — or worse.
