Motorcycle Repair Pricing in Orange, CA

A straight answer about pricing: some work can be estimated quickly, some work needs diagnosis, and no work should start until you understand the plan.

Dusty's pricing rule: quote before we wrench. If something else comes up, you hear about it first. A website guide can explain the pricing factors, but the final estimate depends on the actual bike.

Pricing categories

Quick service

Tires, chain adjustment, oil service, brake pads, simple checks. These are usually easier to estimate once bike/parts are known.

Diagnosis first

No-start, electrical, leaks, weird noises, overheating, running rough. These need testing before a real quote.

Inspection

Used-bike pre-purchase inspections and ride-ready checks. Scope depends on how deep the buyer/rider wants to go.

Custom / rebuild

Vintage work, carburetors, rebuilds, performance changes, and custom projects require a defined scope before pricing.

How to get the most accurate estimate

  • Text the year, make, and model.
  • Explain what changed and when it happens.
  • Send one clear photo or short video.
  • Mention recent service, crash/drop, or modifications.
  • Say whether the bike is currently rideable.

What Dusty's will not do

  • Quote a final repair price from one vague sentence.
  • Start extra work without calling/texting first.
  • Sell phantom repairs just to raise the ticket.
  • Make a rider feel stupid for asking what something costs.

Want a real estimate?

Text year/make/model, what happened, and a photo or video. Dusty will tell you whether it sounds like quick service, diagnosis, inspection, or a shop visit.
