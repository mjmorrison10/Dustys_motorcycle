Pre-Purchase Motorcycle Inspection Checklist

Before you hand over cash for a used bike, slow down and check the stuff that gets expensive fast: title, frame, leaks, tires, brakes, chain, electrical, cold start, and test-ride behavior.

Dusty's rule: If the seller will not let you inspect the bike cold, check the VIN/title, or have a mechanic look at it, walk away.

1. Paperwork & seller check

  • VIN on the frame matches the title.
  • Seller name matches the title, or they can prove legal transfer.
  • No salvage title unless you understand exactly why.
  • Current registration status is clear.
  • Seller has service records, receipts, or at least a credible history.
  • Bike is cold when you arrive — not pre-warmed to hide starting problems.

2. Walkaround red flags

Frame & crash signs

  • Fresh paint on frame or engine cases.
  • Uneven bar ends, pegs, levers, mirrors, or plastics.
  • Scratched engine covers, bent subframe, crooked bars.
  • Fork tubes not parallel or front wheel not centered.

Leaks & fluids

  • Oil around cases, drain plug, filter, fork seals, or shock.
  • Coolant crust or sweet smell near hoses/radiator.
  • Brake fluid dark, low, or leaking at calipers/master cylinder.
  • Fuel smell around tank, petcock, injector rail, or carbs.

3. Wear items that change the real price

  • Tires have date codes under 5 years old and no cracking.
  • Tread is legal and wear is even front/rear.
  • Brake pads have material left; rotors are not deeply grooved.
  • Chain has no tight spots, rust, kinked links, or hooked sprocket teeth.
  • Fork seals are dry after bouncing the front end.
  • Battery starts the bike strongly more than once.

4. Cold start & idle

  • Starts without excessive cranking.
  • No heavy blue smoke after startup.
  • No knocking, clacking, or grinding that gets worse with revs.
  • Idle settles without dying once warm.
  • Throttle snaps back cleanly.
  • Charging voltage is healthy if you can test it.

5. Test ride checks

Only test ride if you are licensed, insured, and the seller agrees. If you cannot test ride, treat that as a pricing risk.
  • Clutch engagement is smooth and does not slip under load.
  • Bike shifts through all gears cleanly.
  • Brakes stop straight without pulsing or grinding.
  • Bike tracks straight with hands lightly on bars.
  • No wobble, headshake, or weird vibration.
  • Temperature stays normal in traffic.

Questions to ask before buying

  • Why are you selling it?
  • When were tires, chain/sprockets, brake pads, and fluids last done?
  • Has it ever been dropped, crashed, tracked, or stunted?
  • Any electrical issues, charging issues, overheating, or oil consumption?
  • Who did the work — you, a shop, or the previous owner?
