## Platform Strategy

### Google Business Profile

**Role:** Local search trust and conversion.

Recommended cadence:

- 2 posts/photos per week.
- Use ride-ready checks, used-bike red flags, real shop photos, and text-first CTA.

Priority actions:

1. Claim/verify GBP.
2. Add 10–15 real shop photos.
3. Post twice weekly.
4. Seed Q&A with text/photo and service questions.
5. Track calls, direction clicks, and website clicks.

---

### Instagram

**Role:** Visual proof and rider engagement.

Recommended cadence:

- 3 feed/reel posts per week.
- Stories 3–5x/week.

Best formats:

- repair clips,
- bike on lift,
- chain/brake/tire close-ups,
- “Would you ride this?” polls,
- quick safety checks,
- text/photo CTA reminders.

---

### Facebook

**Role:** Local trust, older rider audience, community sharing.

Recommended cadence:

- 2 posts per week.
- Cross-post Instagram content with slightly more explanatory captions.
- Keep address and website consistent.

## Content Pillars

1. **Real Repairs** — bike on lift, parts, tools, diagnostics.
2. **No-BS Diagnosis** — what is actually wrong and what can wait.
3. **Ride-Ready Safety** — Santiago Canyon / weekend prep.
4. **Used-Bike Buyer Protection** — pre-purchase inspection education.

## Engagement Rules

- Reply to comments within 24 hours when possible.
- Move diagnosis questions to text: “Text me the year/make/model and a photo.”
- Ask permission before posting identifiable customer bikes, faces, or plates.
- Blur license plates when needed.
- Avoid promising safety clearance from a photo alone.
- Do not argue with bad-faith commenters.
