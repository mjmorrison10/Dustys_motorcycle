# Dusty's Motorcycle Service & Repair — Customer Success Playbook

**Purpose:** Turn one-time repair customers into repeat riders, reviewers, referrers, and long-term shop relationships.

**Core principle:** Customer success for Dusty's is not “checking in.” It is making sure the rider feels safe, informed, respected, and confident after the work.

---

## Customer Success Outcomes

Dusty's should measure customer success by whether riders:

1. understand what was done,
2. know what to watch next,
3. feel the price/work was fair,
4. ride away with confidence,
5. come back for future service,
6. leave a review or refer another rider.

---

## Rider Lifecycle

| Stage | Customer Need | Dusty's Action | Success Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| New inquiry | straight answer | triage + next step | sends bike info/photo |
| Active job | confidence and clarity | quote before wrenching | approves work |
| Pickup | understand repair | explain what was done | rider can repeat summary |
| 2–7 days post-service | confidence after riding | check-in + review ask | positive reply/review |
| 30–60 days | relationship | referral/save-number ask | referral/share |
| 90–180 days | maintenance memory | ride-ready reminder | repeat visit |

---

## Post-Service Handoff Checklist

Before the rider leaves:

- [ ] Explain what was found.
- [ ] Explain what was fixed.
- [ ] Explain what can wait, if anything.
- [ ] Mention any safety items to monitor.
- [ ] Confirm payment/receipt.
- [ ] Ask them to text if anything feels off after the first ride.
- [ ] Log service date, bike, service type, and review eligibility.

Suggested handoff language:

```text
Here’s what I found, here’s what I fixed, and here’s what I’d keep an eye on. If anything feels off after the first ride, text me before you keep riding it.
```

---

## 2–7 Day Check-In

Send after the rider has had a chance to ride.

```text
Hey [Name], just checking in — how’s the [bike] feeling after pickup?

If everything feels right and Dusty treated you fairly, a quick Google review would help local riders find an honest shop:
[Google Review Link]
```

Do **not** ask for a review if:

- the customer has an unresolved issue,
- the bike came back with a complaint,
- the repair outcome is uncertain,
- Dusty has not personally checked in on a sensitive job.

---

## Review Request Workflow

### Eligible

- completed job,
- customer satisfied or neutral-positive,
- no unresolved complaint,
- bike picked up successfully.

### Not eligible yet

- unresolved issue,
- customer upset,
- warranty question open,
- job outcome uncertain.

### Review ask cadence

1. Day 2–7: check-in + review link if positive.
2. Day 10–14: one gentle reminder if no response.
3. Stop after one reminder.

## Referral Workflow

Trigger:

- positive review,
- positive reply,
- repeat customer,
- customer explicitly says they trust Dusty.

Message:

```text
Appreciate you. If you know another rider who needs a straight-answer mechanic in Orange, feel free to send them Dusty’s number: 714-787-9453.

Best first text is year/make/model + what happened + a photo or short video.
```

Optional link:

```text
https://dustysmoto.com/guides/refer-a-rider.html
```

## Customer Health Signals

For a local service business, “health” means relationship strength and repeat/referral potential.

| Signal | Healthy | At Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Post-service reply | says bike feels good | no response or concern |
| Review | leaves positive review | negative/private complaint |
| Repeat behavior | returns for maintenance | disappears after unresolved issue |
| Referral | sends another rider | warns others away |
| Communication | texts with details/photos | vague, frustrated, nonresponsive |

---

## Service Reminder Cadence

Only send if consent/relationship allows.

| Timing | Message Type |
|---|---|
| 30 days | referral/save number ask if positive |
| 90 days | ride-ready reminder |
| 180 days | maintenance check-in |
| Before weekend/season | tires/brakes/chain/fluid reminder |

---

## Complaint Recovery Playbook

If the customer reports a problem:

1. Acknowledge.
2. Ask for details/photo/video.
3. Avoid defensiveness.
4. Offer inspection/next step.
5. Document issue and resolution.
6. Do not ask for review until resolved.

Response template:

```text
Thanks for telling me. I don’t want you riding around worried. Send me what you’re noticing and a photo/video if you can, and let’s figure out the next step.
```

---

## Weekly Customer Success Review

Review weekly:

- completed jobs,
- check-ins sent,
- review requests sent,
- reviews received,
- unresolved issues,
- referral asks sent,
- repeat customers due for reminder.

Questions:

1. Which completed jobs are review-ready?
2. Which customers need follow-up before a review ask?
3. Which happy customers can be asked for a referral?
4. Any issue that needs owner attention?
5. Any pattern that should change the website/FAQ/content?
